    • You might use VoiceThread if...

      You want students to be able to submit timestamped video or text comments on your or other students' videos or presentations.

      Categories: Collaboration Videos

      • VoiceThread

      VoiceThread is a collaborative video application which enables instructors and students to create voice-over presentations. Students can create, share, and collaborate on different multi-media projects that include images, videos, audio files, and other kinds of files. These presentations can then be shared with other students in the course for peer review and discussion. When shared, viewers can interact with these presentations asynchronously through text, audio, or video input. VoiceThread can be integrated within Canvas assignments requiring students to either view a presentation, comment on a presentation, or create a VoiceThread. These assignments connect directly to the Canvas gradebook facilitating grading.

    • You might use ProctorU if...

      You want to have students schedule their quizzes with live, remote proctors that observe the students while they take their quiz.

      Category: Academic Integrity

      • ProctorU

      ProctorU is a service in which a live remote proctor monitors students and their web activity from Chrome or Firefox while they are taking an exam. Instructors can set guidelines for proctors and view recordings later to evaluate specific instances that a proctor flags. When using this service, students will have the option to either take the exam on-demand, meaning whenever they have time, or to register for an appointment ahead of time. Registering ahead of time will ensure that there are enough proctors available.

    • You might use Turnitin if...

      You want to evaluate students' writing for originality.

      Category: Academic Integrity

      • Turnitin

      Turnitin is a plagiarism detection tool that compares student writing submissions to entries in a repository comprised of both papers submitted by other students and online websites/databases. This tool can be used as a standalone tool for students to submit to directly, or can be integrated into assignments within e-Learning (Canvas). Each submission receives a score, allowing instructors to take appropriate action when necessary. Depending on assignment or submission settings, Turnitin allows students to see whether their work is flagged for plagiarism and make necessary adjustments before their final submission. This can build studentsâ€™ skills in self-assessment of their own writing and will help them identify where proper citations are needed. Since Turnitin's default settings save and compare papers to the student repository, be careful with the settings when students are submitting more than one draft of an assignment.

    • You might use Mediasite if...

      You want to record and share lectures with a slide deck that can be viewed simultaneously

      Category: Videos

      • Mediasite

      Mediasite is a tool that can be used to create high-quality videos and streaming content. Mediasite is ideal for highly visual presentations and online lectures, as it allows viewers to simultaneously see the presentation and the speaker. Mediasite videos can be produced through various means, including the MyMediasite desktop recorder, the TechSmith Relay desktop recorder, Mediasite recording appliances found throughout campus classrooms and lecture halls, and the UFIT Academic Media Productions studios (Links to an external site.). Both the MyMediasite desktop recorder and the TechSmith Relay recorder are free to download and use for all UF faculty, staff, and students.
