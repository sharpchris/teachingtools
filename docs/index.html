    • You might use Microsoft Forms if you want to create quick and informal surveys that are saved to Excel.

      Categories: Productivity Classroom Response

      • Microsoft Forms

      Microsoft Forms is a survey and quiz creation tool that can be used in conjunction with Microsoft Excel to collect and analyze data. It may be especially useful for creating end-of-class check-ins or mid-semester feedback forms with your students. Surveys and quizzes created through Forms can be shared with others through Teams or through a generated URL once privacy settings are adjusted. Respondents can be set to public users, anonymous users, or limited to University of Florida members with a Gatorlink.

    • You might use Honorlock if you want to monitor your students' testing environment and computer use during exams but do not want students to have to schedule with a live proctor.

      Category: Academic Integrity

      • Honorlock

      Honorlock is an online proctoring service in which students do not have a live proctor monitoring them throughout their exam. Instead, the student's video and screen are recorded, and the Honorlock AI alerts a live pop-in proctor to intervene when necessary.

    • You might use Perusall if you want to increase student engagement and allow them to create shared annotations on course readings.

      Categories: Collaboration Assessment and Grading

      • Perusall

      One way to increase learner engagement is through group annotation of assigned readings (e.g., articles, textbook). Perusall facilitates a deep reading experience in a learning community by providing an online platform for capturing the comments and questions of peers and the instructor. Perusall can auto-grade student comments (number and content quality (Links to an external site.)) and integrates with the Canvas Gradebook (Links to an external site.).
