    • You might use VoiceThread if...

      You want students to be able to submit timestamped video or text comments on your or other students' videos or presentations.

      Categories: collab video

      • VoiceThread

      VoiceThread is a collaborative video application which enables instructors and students to create voice-over presentations. Students can create, share, and collaborate on different multi-media projects that include images, videos, audio files, and other kinds of files. These presentations can then be shared with other students in the course for peer review and discussion. When shared, viewers can interact with these presentations asynchronously through text, audio, or video input. VoiceThread can be integrated within Canvas assignments requiring students to either view a presentation, comment on a presentation, or create a VoiceThread. These assignments connect directly to the Canvas gradebook facilitating grading.

    • You might use Flipgrid if...

      You want to allow students to post short video responses to topics or questions.

      Category: video

      • Flipgrid

      Creating collaborative learning experiences is imperative to increasing learner engagement and creating a community within your course. Through collaboration, students can work together with peers to negotiate meaning and view topics from different perspectives. Flipgrid is a tool that allows students to collaboratively discuss different topics through video responses to a specific topic. Through this tool, students can easily view and reply to their fellow students' or teacher's posts. From a grading perspective, all student responses are recorded in Speedgrader (Links to an external site.) within Canvas for easy grading.

    • You might use Honorlock if...

      You want a online proctoring solution that scales well and does not require students to schedule appointments.

      Category: integrity

      • Honorlock

      Honorlock is a proctoring service. However, with Honorlock, students do not have a live proctor monitoring them throughout their exam. Instead, the student's video and screen are monitored and recorded, and the Honorlock AI alerts a live pop-in proctor to intervene when necessary.
